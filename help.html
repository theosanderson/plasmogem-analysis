Welcome to the PlasmoGEM phenotype database

The PlasmoGEM project produces high-efficiency vectors for the genetic manipulation of malaria parasites. The project has also developed barcode-sequencing approaches to phenotype parasites generated by these vectors at scale (described in Gomes et al., 2015). These approaches have now provided data on the relative growth rates of asexual blood stages for more than 2,500 Plasmodium berghei genes. This data, yet to be published, is presented on this website.

Data currently available

At present this database contains relative growth rates of determined for asexual blood-stages of gene knock-out mutants in female Balb/c mice. A relative growth rate of 1 reflects the removal of a dispensable gene with no consequence to the parasite under the conditions of the experiment. Growth rate values lower than 1 reflect attenuation of asexual blood-stage growth.

A living dataset

This is an early data release and the experiments, and algorithms, for calculating fitness continue to evolve. The fitness values and phenotypes shown here will change in later releases.

Using the interface

The main graph / table

The fundamental value which this dataset reports is an inferred relative growth rate for the mutant, which is given with a confidence interval reflecting how accurately our algorithm believes it has measured it. The main graph reflects this. The x-axis represents Relative growth rate, with genes likely to be essential on the left hand side and those likely to be redundant, or to accelerate asexual blood stage growth (AP2-G) shown on the right hand-side. The y-axis represents Confidence. Those towards the top we believe we have measured most accurately. At the very bottom of the graph there is total uncertainty about the growth rate.

You can zoom in on the plot by drawing a box with your mouse, and double clicking on it.

You can also click on the Table tab to view the data in tabular format.

Searching

The tabs below the main graph/table allow you to search either by gene ID (can be both the current generation or previous generation of PBANKA IDs) or by a general text search. These searches will filter both the graph and the table.

More information

To gain more information on a gene, please click on one of the points. The gene name, and product description, will be shown. A plot will also illustrate our estimate of the uncertainty in the result. Clicking on More diagnostic info will reveal the raw barcode-counts that went into calculating the growth rate.

What do the phenotypes mean?

To make then numerical values of fitness more digestible we also provide textual descriptions of phenotype. At the moment there are five of these:
Insufficient dataThis means that so few of the barcode for this mutant were observed in the experiment that we cannot call a fitness for the mutant. These genes may be essential, but may sometimes reflect technical failures.
Likely essentialIn these case we observed barcodes falling in abundance and calculated a fitness value that statistically could be the same as that for an essential gene (0-0.1).
Significantly slowThis means that we observed barcodes falling in abundance and calculated a fitness value that is significantly different both from the gene being redundant, and from it being essential
Likely dispensableIn these cases we observed barcode abundance consistent with the gene being dispensable in the blood-stages.
Significantly fastIn these few cases we observed barcode abundance increasing significantly faster than would be expected for a redundant gene knock-out, suggesting accelerated growth in the blood stage.
